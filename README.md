# Beating Heart LED
Beginner friendly tutorial on how to create a Beating Heart LED! <3 @misaprojects on tiktok, project inspired by sagecandence_

## Required components

- 1x 555 timer
- 11x LEDs (any colour)
- 1x 470Ohm resistor
- 1x perf board
- 1x 10uF capacitor
- 1x 68kOhm resistor
- 1x 10kOhm resistor
- 1x push button or on/off button
- 1x 9V battery + snap connector
- Soldering equipment
- Breadboard (optional, for testing)

## Instructions
### Step 1 — Test the Circuit First

Before soldering anything, always test your circuit on a breadboard.

This saves:
- Time
- Effort
- Mistakes in wiring

---

### Step 2 — Breadboard Setup

- A push button is placed after the positive voltage line
- This button is used to turn the circuit ON and OFF
- The circuit is powered using a 9V battery during testing

👉 The breadboard version is only for testing purposes. Final version will be soldered later.

---

### Step 3 — LED Heart Design

- LEDs are arranged in a heart shape on a perfboard (or breadboard for testing)
- The heart shape is created by carefully counting holes on the board
- You can adjust the shape (taller or wider heart) based on your design preference

Tip: Take your time — it took multiple tries to get a good shape

---

### Step 4 — Soldering (Optional / Final Build)

After testing:

1. Copy the breadboard layout onto a perfboard (vero board)
2. Double-check all connections before soldering
3. Carefully solder each connection
4. Avoid overheating components

---

### Step 5 — Power Setup

- Connect a 9V battery for final power
- Ensure correct polarity before powering the circuit

---

### Step 6 — LED Flasher Behavior

The circuit uses an LED flasher effect:

- LEDs flash in a heart pattern
- Flashing starts at a fast speed
- Then gradually slows down
- The cycle repeats continuously

This creates a smooth “pulsing heart” animation effect.

---

### Final Result

Once assembled, press the button to turn the heart ON/OFF
